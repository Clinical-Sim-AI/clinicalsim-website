# Landing Page Framing: PD Remediation Practice Tool

## Page Purpose

A standalone landing page where program directors can sign up for early access to a free/low-cost tool that lets them practice difficult remediation conversations with an AI-simulated resident or fellow. The page serves two goals simultaneously:

1. **Immediate value**: Address PDs' real, documented pain around remediation conversations they were never trained for
2. **Strategic conversion**: Demonstrate the simulation engine's quality so PDs understand what their learners would experience — and become internal champions for institutional adoption

This page should be cross-linked from the existing `/audiences/program-directors` page.

---

## Route

`/practice` or `/pd-practice` (TBD)

---

## Target Audience

Residency and fellowship program directors who:
- Have an upcoming remediation conversation and feel unprepared
- Have had remediation conversations go poorly in the past
- Know they were never formally trained in this specific skill
- Are looking for a way to build confidence before a high-stakes interaction

---

## Tone & Positioning

### What we ARE saying:
- You were trained to be a physician, an educator, and a program leader — remediation conversations are a specific skill the system never taught you
- The data confirms this is a systemic gap: 25% of PDs report little to no training in remediation, and 50% say an accessible toolkit is what they want most
- We built something that lets you practice these conversations on your own terms, on your own time
- When you experience it yourself, you'll understand exactly what your residents and fellows would get

### What we are NOT saying:
- "You're bad at this" (never insult competence)
- "Buy our product" (this page is about early access to a free tool)
- "This replaces your judgment" (it augments, it doesn't replace)

### Positioning line:
> "Practice the conversation before you have the conversation."

### Section 2: "The conversations no one prepared you for"

Brief, empathetic framing section. Not a list of pain points — more of a moment of recognition.

**Content direction:**
You know how to teach a procedure. You know how to run a morbidity conference. But when you sit across from a resident who just learned they're being placed on remediation — or a fellow who's defensive about professionalism feedback — the playbook doesn't exist.

**Scenario examples** (3-4 cards or tiles):
- **Delivering initial remediation news** — The resident doesn't see it coming. They're shocked, defensive, or shut down. You need to be clear, compassionate, and legally sound — all at once.
- **Addressing professionalism concerns** — Nursing staff have reported dismissive behavior. The resident disputes every account. You need to convey the pattern without it becoming adversarial.
- **Communicating milestone deficiency from CCC** — The committee rated them below expected level. They think the assessment is subjective. You need to deliver the message and keep them engaged in a plan.
- **Navigating the conversation after failed remediation** — They completed every step of the plan, but benchmarks weren't met. This is the hardest one because they tried.
- **Discussing performance concerns when impairment may be a factor** — Mental health is a factor in 22-38% of problem residents. You need to address performance without playing doctor.

**Note:** These should feel specific and real. PDs reading this should think "that's exactly the conversation I'm dreading."

---

### Section 3: "See what practice looks like"

This is where we show (not just tell) what the experience is. Even without a working product, we can describe it compellingly.

**What to communicate:**
- You choose a scenario (or describe your own situation)
- You have the conversation with an AI trainee who responds realistically — defensive, emotional, confused, resistant
- The AI doesn't break character or make it easy
- After the conversation, you get structured feedback

**Key emphasis — the feedback and data story:**
- **Real-time milestone-aligned feedback**: Every response is evaluated against ACGME ICS Milestones 2.0
- **Individual quotes highlighted**: Specific moments from your conversation are pulled out with targeted feedback ("Here's where the resident became defensive — here's what happened in your response")
- **Transparency into the framework**: You can see the rubric, the assessment criteria, and the communication framework (Kalamazoo, Calgary-Cambridge) being used
- **Structured documentation**: Every practice session generates a report — the same kind of milestone-aligned documentation that a CCC would use

**Why this matters for the page:**
PDs in academic medicine will immediately ask "how does this assess?" and "what framework are you using?" Showing the assessment architecture builds trust and differentiates from generic AI chatbots. This is also the moment where the PD starts thinking "wait — this feedback and documentation would be incredibly useful for my learners too."

---

### Section 4: "Full transparency into our approach"

This section addresses the academic medicine buyer's core objection: "How do I know what the AI is doing?"

**What to show:**
- The case library — PDs can see exactly what scenarios exist, what communication skills each one targets, and how they map to milestones
- The assessment rubric — not a black box; the criteria are visible and grounded in established communication frameworks
- The feedback methodology — how individual moments are identified, scored, and translated into actionable guidance
- How data is tracked longitudinally — progress across multiple practice sessions, trend lines, specific areas of improvement

**Framing:**
> "We don't ask you to trust a black box. Every scenario, every rubric, every piece of feedback is visible, editable, and grounded in the communication science your CCCs already use."

**What the full platform offers (brief, not exhaustive):**
- Unlimited AI patient encounters across clinical communication scenarios
- Milestone-aligned assessment and documentation for every session

**CTA:** "Learn more about ClinicalSim for your program" → links to `/audiences/program-directors` or the main product page

**Important:** This section should be ~20% of the page. The other 80% is about the PD's own experience. Don't dilute the personal value with an institutional sales pitch.
